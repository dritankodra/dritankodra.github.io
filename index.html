About Me

I am a Data Scientist / AI Engineer with a strong background in Physics and Astronomy.
I received my Bachelor's degree in Physics from the Aristotle University of Thessaloniki (AUTh) in 2010, and my Master's degree in Computational Physics from the same institution in 2012. I continued my academic career with the PhD program in Physics and Astronomy at the University of Pittsburgh (UPitt) in Pennsylvania, US, earning my title in 2018.

Following my PhD, I held the position of postdoctoral researcher collaborating with the Biomic_AUTh research group at the Department of Chemistry of AUTh, contributing to a variety of projects such as the design, development and deployment of food composition databases, the meta-study of literature on issues regarding metabolite annotations using text mining techniques, as well as the development of Machine Learning models for predictions of retention times from Liquid Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (LC-MS) experiments. I had simultaneously been employed as the Lead Data Analyst by the Market Research company "To the point", where my responsibilities entailed the design and implementation of Market Research and Public Opinion Surveys, as well as the collection and analysis of data, leading the efforts of developing in-house computational and statistical tools of automation.

For the past 3.5 years, I have been honing my expertise as a Data Scientist and AI Engineer at Pfizer's Center for Digital Innovation, where I continue to apply advanced machine learning and AI techniques to solve complex business challenges. I combine rigorous scientific methodology with cutting-edge AI technologies to develop innovative solutions and extract meaningful insights from complex datasets.

Outside of my professional responsibilities, I enjoy exploring the latest developments in AI and continuously learning new tools and topics to stay at the forefront of the field.
